What the grade measures

Letter grades summarize how much framing and bias-signal load your scans found — not “truth” or political party.

v2 calibrated neutrality: severity × confidence × voice (quotes count less) · content-type aware · still techniques only.

Each real scan gets a neutrality score from 0–100. Outlets and journalists keep a running average of those scores. The average becomes a letter grade so you can compare at a glance, then open the full audit history for evidence.

Same grades, two scoreboards

Privacy-first · chrome.storage.local only · no article body stored

Outlets by host

Every qualifying scan rolls into a running average for that news site’s hostname.

  • Leaderboards: most unbiased · most biased · recent
  • Letter badge from average neutrality across samples
  • Click a row → full local audit history for that outlet
  • Stored under keys like bn_site_*

Journalists by byline

Author names are parsed from the byline and graded with the identical A+–F scale.

  • “By Jane Doe and John Smith” → two separate scoreboards
  • Letter badge + sample count for each person
  • Click a name → every stored audit credited to them
  • Stored under keys like bn_jour_*

Not a politics meter

Grades reflect rhetorical / framing load detected in your scans — not left vs right, and not a claim that a story is true or false.

Local only

Scores live on this device. Settings → clear data wipes outlet stats, journalist stats, and scan history together.

Evidence first

The letter is a shortcut. Open any outlet or journalist to expand past reports: overview, caveats, and signal list.
